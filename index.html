MyName: LiGao email: zeli.gl@ant-intl.com My research has focused on several key areas within the domain of large-scale language models, encompassing the entire development lifecycle to enhance their efficiency, capability, and applicability. Specifically, my work delves into: Large Model Pre-training: Investigating innovative architectures, training methodologies, and data curation strategies to build robust foundational models from scratch. This includes research on parameter-efficient training techniques, optimizing compute and data utilization, and improving model generalization across diverse domains and languages. Inference Optimization: Developing and implementing advanced techniques to drastically reduce the computational latency and resource footprint during model deployment. This encompasses research into model compression (e.g., pruning, quantization), knowledge distillation, efficient attention mechanisms, and hardware-aware acceleration to enable faster and more cost-effective inference at scale. Post-Training Processes: Exploring critical stages that occur after pre-training to align models with specific tasks and human preferences. My research in this area covers supervised fine-tuning (SFT), reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), safety alignment to mitigate biases and harmful outputs, and evaluation methodologies for assessing model robustness and fairness. Automated Optimization of Prompts or Skills: Pioneering methods for the autonomous discovery, refinement, and management of effective prompts and reusable “skills” for large models. This involves research in prompt engineering automation, automated few-shot learning, self-debugging prompts, and the development of systems that can learn to optimally interact with and guide LLMs to solve complex, multi-step problems with minimal human intervention. By integrating these interconnected facets, my research aims to push the boundaries of what large language models can achieve, making them more powerful, efficient, controllable, and accessible for real-world applications.
